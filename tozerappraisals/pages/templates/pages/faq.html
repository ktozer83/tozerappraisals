Is the property assessment value similar to the current market value?

Not necessarily: MPAC property assessors do not normally inspect house interiors. What if the home has been upgraded - or neglected? Or there has been a freeze on reassessments? Or market and economic conditions have changed since the last assessment date?

Will the appraised value of a property be different depending on who the Client is - be it buyer, seller or the lender?

Not so. The appraiser has no vested interest in the value outcome - regardless of who the Client is.

Does market value roughly equal the replacement cost contained in an insurance policy?

Rarely: Replacement cost is the dollar amount required to reconstruct a property in-kind - an insurance replacement cost includes demolition costs, dump fees, etc. but does not include the land value.

Do appraisers use a formula, such as a specific price per square foot, to estimate the value of a home?

This approach is valuable when appraising a new home. But for an older home, purchasers don't make an offer to purchase based on the cost per square foot as if new.

Can you often tell what a property is worth simply by looking at the outside?

Appraising a property by looking at it from the outside would be the same as buying a car without at least taking it for a test drive. There are many factors inside the home to consider that have a significant influence on value - as well as other economic factors such as market trends, interest rate movements and so on.

Is the homeowner entitled to a copy of the report even though the report was done for the lender, even though the homeowner paid for it?

The appraiser is not at liberty to provide a copy of the report to any other entity other than the Client. So unless they release their interest in the document, the lender essentially "owns" the appraisal and retains the exclusive right to use the report or any information contained within.

Is an appraisal value different from a realtor's value?

Both use similar methods of arriving at value estimates. If the market analysis is done correctly, then the value estimates should be reasonably close. However, there are circumstances where the two may be different. An example may be where a single family residence is used as a student residence or a duplex is used as a triplex. Often the listing and eventual selling price of those properties will be influenced by the income derived. If highest and best use of the property is determined by the appraiser to be single family residential only, the appraisal value may be different. Also, there are instances where chattels (i.e. furnishings, hottubs, and even vehicles) are included in the purchase price. Mortgage appraisals normally do not include the effect of chattels.
